About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Associate in the Department of Statistics and Data Science at Yale University, under the supervision of Professor Xiang Zhou. Previously, I was a postdoctoral research fellow in the Department of Biostatistics at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor (2024-2025). I received my Ph.D. in Statistics from Peking University in 2024, under the supervision of Professor Minghua Deng.

My research focuses on developing statistical methods and deep learning approaches for analyzing spatial transcriptomics and single-cell multi-omics data. I am particularly interested in spatial domain detection, gene colocalization analysis, and novel cell detection.
